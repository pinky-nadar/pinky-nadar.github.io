Wimo App

Wimo lets you take opinions on your favorite future events with a simple Yes or No. As the event outcome is declared, you validate your opinion or figure out the truth with rewards!

Why do we need?

Gen-z and millennial have knowledge on various on-going topics that are happening worldwide. They all have their own opinions which they share while having a discussion or at any public gatherings.

Wimo provides a platform where you can trade on your opinions and earn rewards. This opinions are taken on any future event that will happen. To trade a user will need an opponent who has different opinion. When the future event announces result the winner takes all.

Goals

  • Fun and Casual
  • Gain User Trust
  • Seamless Transaction
  • User Engagement

Process

Started the project communicating with client on their requirements and based on that I started researching about the case. I gathered few user insights from the competitors apps. What they are good at and what needs to be improved. Based on that ideas were created and designs were made. Finally, the designs were tested to the user groups and we received feedback.

Target Audience


Research

We had brainstorming session with client & team during the initial stage of the project, where I tried to understand the user base, business goals what are going to be the expectations from each one of us for this project.

Research data was shared by the client & I studied that to utilize in the project.

I did competitor analysis, the apps that are working in the same category, noted their plus points & minus points. And how Wimo can offer more then them.

Storyboarding


Mind Mapping

Started with mind mapping, we had our core feature which is to select an event and trade. Kept this core in center and I gathered branches based on that. And further divided them to sub tasks which in the end became a hierarchical & interconnected structure.

It’s a creative & logical way to brainstorm which helped me to generate, structure & analyze ideas visually.

Additionally, it helps to move ahead with the next step which is information architechture.

Wireframes

Picked up the core flow of the app & started to begin with it since the flow will be done on recurring basis by the user so, we thought to prioritize this flow & have seamless experience.

Once the core wire-frame built, I converted it to interactive prototype and tested with few users to gather their insights.

The overall feedback was good. One of the most common feedback was to give a bit context about the flow for the users who doesn’t have any trading knowledge. Later we improvised on this feedback in the onboarding part.

Moodboard

Mood board helps establishing the visual direction of the product. I gathered various visual elements, such colors, textures, images & typography. With these I explored different styles & aesthetics.
