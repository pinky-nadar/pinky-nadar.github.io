Turning Cold Calls into Warm Conversations

Ever made 100 cold calls in a day?

Imagine making back-to-back calls, trying to sound confident, but scrambling for customer details across multiple tools. By the time you find what you need, the moment is gone.

That was the daily struggle for RMs at Blox.xyz—frustrating and inefficient. We had to fix this—not just to simplify their work, but to help them turn calls into leads.

Understanding the Pain Points

Before jumping into solutions, we had to listen. We sat down with five RMs and their team lead, and the conversations were eye-opening:

  • I waste so much time searching for customer details.
  • I have no clue what a customer needs unless I dig through old notes.
  • Cold calling is exhausting. It’s hard to stay motivated.

Their frustrations boiled down to three core issues:

  • Too many disconnected tools → Customer data was scattered across different platforms.
  • No structured follow-ups → They struggled to remember key details after calls.
  • Low engagement, high burnout → Making 100+ calls a day felt like a never-ending grind.


Looking at the Competition

To ensure our design was both competitive and relevant, I researched industry-leading apps like Knowlarity, Ameyo, and Aircall. These apps are well-known for their communication and call management features, and studying them gave us insights into common pain points and best practices. However, we identified several areas where these platforms could be enhanced, particularly in terms of user engagement and ease of use during high-pressure situations like cold calling.

Key takeaways from research


From Scribbles to Strategy

Armed with these insights, we jumped into a whiteboarding session. And trust me, if you saw our board, you’d think it was pure chaos. But hidden in those messy sketches were the building blocks of something great.

  • What if we brought all customer data onto a single screen? No more endless tab-switching.
  • What if we made follow-ups effortless? AI-powered call transcripts could highlight key customer insights automatically.
  • What if we actually made cold calling… fun? A leaderboard and small rewards could turn the grind into a game.

We were onto something. Now, it was time to validate our ideas.
